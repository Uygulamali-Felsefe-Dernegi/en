  • What is Applied Philosophy?

    Applied philosophy aims to use philosophical thought to provide practical solutions to ethical and social problems encountered in everyday life. By effectively applying this discipline in various fields, our association aims to approach the challenges faced by individuals and societies from a philosophical perspective. Applied philosophy, which reminds us that philosophical thinking is effective in all areas of life, aims to think critically, make ethical decisions and create social awareness.

    Applied philosophy, such as environmental ethics, bioethics, and philosophy of education, offers solutions to social problems and explores how ethical principles can be integrated into social life. Sub-disciplines such as technology, law, gender and philosophy of art offer in-depth evaluations on various topics. Our association aims to raise awareness of intellectual and ethical problems in these fields.

    The goal of our association is to emphasize the role of philosophical thinking in our daily lives and to address ethical and social issues in various areas of society. Our educational programs and events aim to show how philosophical approaches can be applied to social problems. In addition, through projects in various fields, we aim to demonstrate the potential of philosophical thinking to improve our social values and quality of life.

  • Paternalistic Artificial Intelligence: The Case of Elderly Care

    The abstract of the article by Cristina Voinea, Tenzin Wangmo and Constantin Vică discusses an important issue of how AI is being used in elderly care. The paper details AI's approach to intervening in the elderly by prioritizing their well-being rather than fulfilling their wishes. This approach aims to protect the safety and health of the elderly by limiting their decisions, but also raises ethical debates as it restricts their autonomy.

  • Why Are Some People Happy When They Die?

    Mattias Tranberg's article "Why Some People Are Happy When Dying" explores why some people can be happy when death approaches. The article explores Simon Boas' positive emotions during the dying process and the psychological dynamics behind these experiences. Boas candidly expresses his happiness as death approaches and reflects on how this process can bring more meaning and fulfillment. The article offers an important perspective on how facing death and the emotional changes at the end of life can lead individuals to appreciate life more and find meaning. It also includes research on the impact of palliative care and emotional experiences during this process.

  • Humanities on a Burned Planet

    This article questions the role of the humanities in light of today's crises - war, pandemic and climate change. It discusses the importance of aesthetics and art through Vincent van Gogh's painting "Mulberry Tree" and emphasizes the value of the humanities in the face of crises. The author discusses the necessity of seeking truth and beauty even in difficult times, and how this endeavor offers humanity a refuge against existential threats. Brian Treanor's essay offers a profound perspective for those seeking to understand the importance of the humanities in times of crisis.
